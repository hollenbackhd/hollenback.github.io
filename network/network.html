Greek and Norse Gods:
Family Ties of the Gods:
These tangled trees represent the most well-known and influential families of Greek and Norse mythology.
The first notable difference between the two families is the "tight-knit" relationship the Greek Gods share.
The deceitful and violent relationships that Zeus has with his female family members represent the King of God's ultimate power and influence in the family.
The relationships between the Norse Gods are less incestuous, although it does occur between Angrboða and her Nephew Loki as well Njörd and his sister siring Freyr and Freya.
These relationships are minimal and are not as widespread as Zeus' influence among his family.
This difference in relationships may be due to the fact that the Greek Gods and immortal, while the Norse Gods are not.
Therefore, the Greek gods are less like the humans that worship them, and the morals and laws that the mortal humans abide by do not affect or influence them in the way that they influence the Norse Gods who have a more direct relationship with their human followers.
Patriarchs of the Gods:
Another notable aspect of these family trees is the Patriarchs. For the Greek Gods, there is one creator, Gaia who created her husband Ouranos and together created the pantheon of gods.
The Norse Patriarchs are Ymir and Auðumbla. These two Gods essentially create two distinct families that grow and interact with each other later in the tree.
